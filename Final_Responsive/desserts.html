New Haven Pizzas

New Haven bar pie is a distinct, ultra-thin, and crispy-crust pizza style often found in Connecticut, characterized by its "cracker-thin" dough, limited toppings, and often, a slightly charred, chewy texture.Traditional New Haven-style pizza, known locally as apizza (pronounced ah-beets), is a thin-crust, coal-fired pie characterized by its charred, chewy crust, irregular shape, and minimal toppings. Originating from Italian immigrant roots, it features a prominent tomato sauce, garlic, oregano, and pecorino romano, usually with light mozzarella.

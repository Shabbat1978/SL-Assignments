Chicago Pizzas

Chicago-style deep-dish pizza originated in 1943, created by Ike Sewell and Ric Riccardo at Pizzeria Uno. Designed as a substantial "knife-and-fork" meal, it features a thick, buttery crust, layers of mozzarella cheese, and chunky tomato sauce on top. While deep-dish is famous, Chicago "tavern-style" thin-crust pizza actually originated earlier to accompany bar drinks

History of Deep Dish Pizza

Ike Sewell and Ric Riccardo founded Pizzeria Uno in 1943, creating the deep-dish style, sometimes attributed to chef Rudy Malnati Sr.. The "Upside-Down" Structure: To prevent the thick layer of cheese from burning during the long baking time, the pizza is built in reverse: dough on the bottom, cheese next, followed by fillings, and topped with sauce.
